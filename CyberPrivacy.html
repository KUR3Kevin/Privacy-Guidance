Section 01

Software Foundation

Before applying behavioral changes, you must secure your gateway to the internet. These three tools form the bedrock of any serious privacy setup.

Brave Browser

Blocks fingerprinting, cross-site trackers, and ads out of the box. Built on Chromium, stripped of Google's telemetry.

Tor Browser

The gold standard for anonymity. Routes traffic through 3 random nodes. Prevents traffic analysis at the network level.

DuckDuckGo

A search engine that builds no profile on you. Escapes the filter bubble of manipulated search results.

Section 02

Beyond Cookies

Photometric and Exif Data

Every photo contains hidden metadata embedded by your device:

  • GPS Coordinates — exact location where the photo was taken.
  • Device ID — the model and serial number of your camera.
  • Timestamp — validates your location at a precise moment.

Use Scrambled Exif (Android) or Metapho (iOS) to strip data before uploading.

Link Decoration Tracking

Marketers append UTM codes to links to track you across platforms.

Example: website.com?utm_source=facebook&user_id=12345

Always delete everything after the ? in a URL before sharing it.

Section 03

Browser Hardening

  • 1. Use Brave or Firefox — avoid pure Chrome.
  • 2. Install uBlock Origin — the best ad and script blocker.
  • 3. Install Privacy Badger — learns to block invisible trackers.
  • 4. HTTPS Everywhere — force encrypted connections.
  • 5. Disable Third-Party Cookies — in browser settings.
  • 6. Clear Cookies on Exit — set browser to auto-wipe.
  • 7. Strict Tracking Protection — enable in browser settings.
  • 8. Disable WebRTC — prevents IP leaks through the browser.
  • 9. Randomize User Agent — appear as a different device.
  • 10. Sandbox Containers — isolate Facebook and Google tabs.
  • 11. Disable Autofill — don't let browsers save addresses.
  • 12. Turn Off Telemetry — opt out of all usage data.
  • 13. Avoid Browser Password Managers — they are often targeted.

Section 04

Network & Wi-Fi

  • 14. Use a Reputable VPN — Mullvad, ProtonVPN, or IVPN.
  • 15. Avoid Free VPNs — they sell your data to pay for servers.
  • 16. Use Tor for Sensitive Searches — onion routing for maximum privacy.
  • 17. Change DNS Settings — use Cloudflare 1.1.1.1 or Quad9.
  • 18. Enable DNS over HTTPS — encrypts your DNS requests.
  • 19. Hide Wi-Fi SSID — make your home network invisible.
  • 20. Guest Network for IoT — isolate smart devices on a separate network.
  • 21. Disable UPnP — turn this off in your router settings.
  • 22. Change Router Admin Credentials — never use admin/admin.
  • 23. Disable Bluetooth When Idle — prevents beacon tracking.
  • 24. Forget Old Wi-Fi Networks — prevents rogue hotspot connections.
  • 25. MAC Address Randomization — enable in mobile Wi-Fi settings.

Section 05

Accounts & Passwords

  • 26. Use a Password Manager — Bitwarden or KeePassXC.
  • 27. Unique Passwords — never reuse a single password anywhere.
  • 28. 2FA via TOTP App — use Aegis or Raivo, never SMS.
  • 29. Hardware Keys — YubiKey for critical accounts like email and banking.
  • 30. Passkeys — use biometric passkeys wherever supported.
  • 31. Email Aliases — use SimpleLogin or Apple Hide My Email.
  • 32. HaveIBeenPwned — regularly check if your email is in a breach.
  • 33. Recovery Codes — print them and store them offline.
  • 34. Fake Security Answers — lie on security questions. Save the lie in your manager.
  • 35. Login Notifications — enable alerts for any new logins.
  • 36. Close Old Accounts — reduce your attack surface by deleting unused accounts.

Section 06

Device Hardening

  • 37. Full Disk Encryption — BitLocker on Windows, FileVault on Mac.
  • 38. Keep OS Updated — patch zero-day exploits immediately.
  • 39. Use a Standard User Account — don't run as Admin daily.
  • 40. Enable Firewall — built-in OS firewalls are highly effective.
  • 41. Disable Location Services — globally or restrict per app.
  • 42. Cover Your Webcam — physical sticker or slider cover.
  • 43. Mic Lock — use a hardware blocker or software switch.
  • 44. Remove Bloatware — uninstall all pre-loaded junk apps.
  • 45. Disable Find My Device — if location privacy is critical to you.
  • 46. Consider Linux — Mint or Fedora for significantly better privacy.
  • 47. Windows Defender — sufficient protection if kept updated.

Data Brokers

  • 48. Opt-Out Services — use DeleteMe or Incogni to scrub data brokers.
  • 49. Manual Opt-Out — search your name and request removal yourself.
  • 50. Fake Name for Deliveries — use a name variation for packages.

  • 56. Freeze Your Credit — stop identity theft at the credit bureau level.

Section 08

Mobile Security

  • 57. Signal Messenger — end-to-end encrypted texts and calls.
  • 58. Disappearing Messages — set all chats to auto-delete.
  • 59. Disable Ad ID — reset or fully delete your Advertising ID.
  • 60. Audit App Permissions — does a calculator need your contacts? No.
  • 61. Hide Lock Screen Notifications — conceal message content from view.
  • 62. 6-Digit or Alphanumeric PIN — 4 digits are too easy to brute force.
  • 63. Scrambled PIN Pad — prevents shoulder surfing attacks.
  • 64. Biometrics Caution — a password can't be legally forced; a fingerprint can.
  • 65. GrapheneOS — consider a de-Googled Android operating system.
  • 66. Faraday Bags — block all wireless signals when traveling.

Section 09

Social & Behavioral

  • 67. Zero Trust — verify every link, even from trusted contacts.
  • 68. Hover Links — check the real URL destination before clicking.
  • 69. Caution with QR Codes — they can instantly route to malicious sites.
  • 70. Callback Verification — if a bank calls you, hang up and call back on the official number.
  • 71. Social Media Silence — never post vacation photos while you are still away.
  • 72. Reverse Image Search — check regularly if your photos are being misused.
  • 73. Scrub Metadata — remove Exif data from photos before posting online.
  • 74. Be Boring Online — the less you stand out, the less you get targeted.
  • 75. Compartmentalize — never link your gaming identity to your professional one.

Section 10

Financial & Physical

  • 76. Virtual Credit Cards — use Privacy.com to generate merchant-specific card numbers.
  • 77. Cash is King — physical cash leaves absolutely no digital trail.
  • 78. RFID Wallet — block card skimmers in public places.
  • 79. Shred Documents — cross-cut shred all mail before throwing it away.
  • 80. Privacy Screen Filter — block visual hacking on your laptop and phone in public.
